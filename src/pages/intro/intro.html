Welcome to SandySearch!

Croud-sourced Emergency Services for During and After a Disaster

I was in Super Storm Sandy in 2012, and was without power for 9 days.

After the storm, we had a very hard time discovering what services (gasoline, food, water, ice, power, ATMs) were available (both when and where).

And if you have only a half a tank of gas, you don't want to drive around looking for more, without knowing where to go.

How it works

The idea for this service is to use the power of crowdsourcing (think "Waze for Services, after a disaster").

It currently allows anyone (without a login) to search and report on services available in their area.

Verified users (pre-registered, think Red Cross, National Guard, and business owners/managers) can leave "verified" service status updates, at no cost.

All usage is anonymous, if not logged in.

All of our features

Here's what you can do with SandySearch

  • Search for specific types of services near you

  • Comment on listed services

  • Add new services by address or your location

  • See services around you, on a map

You will need a working network connection to use this!
